## Overall Impression

HTCalc has a credible product-UI foundation and the right split-pane workbench model. The highest-value opportunity is not visual decoration; it is reducing RFQ overload and making validation/recovery more traceable at the field and section level.

## What's Working

- The product register is strong: restrained palette, compact controls, flat workbench surfaces, and minimal decorative behavior match `PRODUCT.md` and `DESIGN.md`.
- The traceability foundation is real: warnings, validation checks, imported assumptions, cost breakdowns, reports, project save/load, and RFQ presets are all present.
- The split input/result layout is the right base pattern for repeated engineering and estimating review.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] RFQ Cognitive Overload

**Why it matters:** Estimators are making commercial decisions, but the RFQ screen exposes source choice, lot basis, preset controls, 11 shop-rate fields, 9 overrides, adjustments, and output confidence too early.

**Fix:** Make RFQ a staged workbench: Source and imported assumptions, Pricing basis, Rates, Adjustments, Review. Collapse advanced overrides by default and group rates into labor, equipment, fees, and margin.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill src/ui/main.ts`

### [P1] Field-Local Validation and Recovery Are Too Weak

**Why it matters:** In a calculation tool, a panel-level error without a clear path back to the exact field undermines trust.

**Fix:** Add inline validation messages, section error badges, and actionable empty states. Keep the last valid recommendation visible with a clear stale state when current inputs are invalid.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden src/ui/main.ts`

### [P2] Critical Actions Rely Too Much on Icons and Native Dialogs

**Why it matters:** Save/load/report/export/delete are high-value actions, but icon-only clusters and browser prompt/confirm feel less deliberate and less accessible.

**Fix:** Use labeled grouped actions or menus for reports and projects. Replace preset prompt/delete confirm with app-native dialogs that include focus trap, Esc, cancel, and explicit consequences.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify src/ui/main.ts`

### [P2] Confidence and Warning Reassurance Needs More Explanation

**Why it matters:** A green/yellow/red chip says status, not why the model chose that status or what to do next.

**Fix:** Add a compact confidence explainer beside the chip: top drivers, unresolved validation burden, and “starting point, not qualification” language.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish src/ui/main.ts`

### [P2] Mobile/Collapsed Result Access Likely Degrades

**Why it matters:** When the layout collapses, users may edit long forms before seeing the recommendation, warnings, or quote confidence.

**Fix:** Add mobile anchors or a sticky “View recommendation” control, keep active confidence visible, and verify tab/action wrapping at narrow widths.

**Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt src/ui/main.ts`

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex, power user:** No keyboard shortcuts or command palette for save/load/report/settings. RFQ rate editing is one-field-at-a-time. Settings and report dialogs do not show obvious Esc/focus-return behavior from source.

**Jordan, first-timer:** Default results appear quickly, but the first “what should I change?” path is unclear. AI, CSR, CP, austemperability, and imported assumptions need better explanation than occasional hover help.

**Sam, accessibility-dependent user:** Focus styling exists, but CSS pseudo-tooltips may not expose help text to screen readers. Dialog focus management is not evident. Icon-only actions depend on title/ARIA quality.

**Morgan, estimator/metallurgist:** Needs a tighter audit trail. Imported process assumptions, calibration, rate preset provenance, validation status, and internal/customer report boundaries should be more explicit before a quote is exported.

## Minor Observations

- “No active risk warnings” may sound too absolute; “No model-triggered warnings” is safer.
- Project status is right-aligned under the header and can be missed.
- Settings reset changes units and ADI calibration together without a stronger checkpoint.
- Repeated uppercase section headings plus icons create visual sameness.
- Simulated tables using div roles need a deeper accessibility audit.
- Palette drift advisories should be resolved by either adding the extra colors to `DESIGN.md` or remapping them to existing tokens.

## Questions to Consider

- Should RFQ start as a quote-review sequence instead of a dense pricing spreadsheet?
- What must always be visible for a metallurgist to trust the recommendation?
- Are AI and CSR primary user concepts, or should they move behind a diagnostics layer?
- What should the app say when a user exports a report with unchecked validation items?
- Is the confidence chip a result, a warning, or a workflow state? Right now it is trying to be all three.
