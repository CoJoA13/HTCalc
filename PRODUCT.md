# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HTCalc serves a mix of heat-treatment engineers, metallurgists, estimators, and shop managers who need a traceable first-pass workbench for process planning and heat-treatment service RFQ review. They are using the app in engineering and estimating contexts where the main job is to compare inputs, understand warnings, preserve project state, and produce reviewable process or quote outputs without implying formal qualification.

## Product Purpose

HTCalc provides deterministic heat-treatment process recommendations and RFQ pricing support for ADI, steel austempering, martempering, and heat-treatment service estimating. Success means users can enter or import recipe assumptions, review calculated windows and warnings, adjust shop-specific pricing inputs, save or share project files, and export reports while keeping the limitations and validation burden clear.

## Brand Personality

Technical, restrained, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like an engineering workbench: direct, dense enough for repeated use, and explicit about uncertainty, validation, and commercial boundaries.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-site polish, flashy dashboards, decorative motion, gamified visuals, and anything that makes calculations feel less traceable. Do not make the app feel like an accounting system, CRM, production traveler, certification tool, or black-box AI recommender.

## Design Principles

- Preserve traceability: make inputs, assumptions, warnings, and report outputs easy to inspect and connect.
- Stay operational: favor compact, predictable controls and repeat-use workflows over promotional presentation.
- Signal confidence honestly: warnings, validation checks, and model limitations should be visible without overwhelming the primary workflow.
- Separate planning from qualification: keep language and UI states clear that recommendations are starting points for review, calibration, and validation.
- Respect shop-specific work: preserve local rates, project files, and user-entered assumptions without surprising mutation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Controls should be keyboard-friendly with visible focus states. Status, warning, and validation meaning should not rely on color alone. Motion should be minimal and respect reduced-motion preferences.
